Faith & Ethics

Christian Ethics in Technology

My approach to ethics starts with my faith. I believe every person is made in the image of God, which means the systems I help build should protect people, not use them. That applies to how data is handled, how decisions are made, and how technology is used in real life.

In practical terms, that means being honest about what systems can and can’t do, protecting personal information, and refusing shortcuts that might compromise integrity. I want my work in technology to reflect Christ-like character: truthfulness, humility, and care for the most vulnerable users.

Whether I am designing software, working with data, or troubleshooting systems, I want my choices to be shaped by loving God and loving my neighbor. For me, “good tech” isn’t just about performance or features, but about whether it serves people well and honors God in the process.

23 “Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord, not for human masters,”

Colossians 3:23 (NIV)
